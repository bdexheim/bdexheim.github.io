Burst - Lighting Design Charrette

Burst is a side-table/stool that functions as an ambient light, adapting to its environment based on the surrounding light.

Role

CAD modeler, manufacturing, lighting design and implementation

Collaborators

Caleigh O'Donnell, Richa Sharma, Funmilayo Makinde

Timeline

2 weeks

Methods and Tools

Methods: rapid prototyping

Tools: Shapr3D, manufacturing, Arduino

Design Process

During a design charrette, the team was tasked with designing and creating a functioning light. The end result was a side-table/stool that functions as an ambient light which changes with its environment.

After brainstorming over 20 ideas, the team converged on this concept and prototyped with a transparent trash can and cardboard.

I created the CAD model and refined the design specifics, participated in the woodwork, and individually developed the electronics and software for the light including the auto-dimming functionality.

Results

Reflections

Prototyping to learn, both lofi and fully functioning, can be very influential. In some scenarios, it can be helpful to jump into prototyping ASAP rather than continuing too long with user research.
