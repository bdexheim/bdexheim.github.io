Chatterbox - Future of Work

Privacy for virtual, ad-hoc connections

An exploration for the Future of Work (2-3 years out). Chatterbox provides easy access to privacy for ad-hoc connections with co-workers and clients located across the globe without the in-office distractions.

Role

Designer

Collaborators

Solo

Timeline

4 weeks

Methods and Tools

Methods: rapid prototyping, primary and secondary user research

Tools: Shapr3D, prototyping with chipboard, cardboard

Research Process

During this Future of Work project, I performed primary and secondary user research to identify opportunities for workplace products in the next 2-3 years. In addition, I reflected on my own experiences working with globally dispersed teams, but also returning to office on a hybrid basis.

After brainstorming many opportunities, I converged on providiing privacy for ad-hoc virtual connections while in the office and began ideating.

Design and Prototyping Process

Next, I began exploring exploring solutions based on inspiration and through sketching and CAD modeling.

From there, I built small-scale models as well as a lofi full-scale model to learn. This influenced the dimensions and form as we were able to sense what it truly would feel like to use the full-scale product.

Prototyping

Product Concept Positioning

During the research and design process, I continued to reflect on the question,

where does this concept fit within the ecosystem of office collaboration products and environments?

Presentation

Lastly, I presented the concept with a narrative along with a refined small-scale model.

Reflections

"Sticky" stories are crucial for buy-in to design concepts.

Early prototyping, including lofi, full-scale, can be influential in understanding the true feel of a concept.
