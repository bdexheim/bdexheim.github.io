Inclusive Design for Education

Designing an immersive experience using sight (VR), hearing (VR), and touch (Arduino and sensors), while provoking curiosity about other geographic regions.

Role

VR and immersive experience designer, software and electronics developer

Collaborators

Shurthi Iyer, Shimama Zainab

Timeline

4 weeks

Methods and Tools

Methods: inclusive design, rapid prototyping

Tools: A-Frame, Arduino

Design Process

Influenced by Dr. Bess Williamson and Kat Holmes, among others, our team sought to design an inclusive education lesson about seasonal weather patterns.

I specifically focused on designing and prototyping an integrated immersive experience through a VR experience and an Arduino + sensor add-on.

Immersive Experience

VR immersive exerpience functions on desktop and Google Cardboard.

User can use gaze-based selection to transport to different scenes, where audio accompanies the visual setting. Simultaneously, the Arduino-powered peltier plates and motor-controlled fans simulate the temperature and wind.

In this clip, a user is transported to Chicago in winter, right next to the Bean.

Reflections and Next Steps

Vision is not the only sense. By incorporating more immersive vision and other senses like audio touch can not only make the experience more inclusive, but also more immersive for all. In addition, immersing users into other geographies can provide a sense of what life may be like there while provoking curiosity.

The experience could be expanded so that students could actually capture and share their environment with others around the globe.
